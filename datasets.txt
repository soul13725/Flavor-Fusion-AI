1) RecipeNLG Dataset (2M+ recipes)

A massive semi-structured recipe dataset with titles, ingredients, and instructions.

➡️ Kaggle – RecipeNLG:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/paultimothymooney/recipenlg

(There’s also a variant at https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/saldenisov/recipenlg
 which mirrors it with slightly different provisions.)

You can also find a version indexed on Hugging Face:
https://huggingface.co/datasets/mbien/recipe_nlg

2) Food.com Recipes and Interactions Dataset

Contains recipes from Food.com with user interaction info, ingredient lists, and descriptive text.

➡️ Kaggle – Food.com Recipes and Interactions:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shuyangli94/food-com-recipes-and-user-interactions

3) Epicurious Recipes (with Nutrition Info)

About 20k recipes with nutrition breakdown (calories, protein, fat, etc.), categories, and ratings.

➡️ Kaggle – Epicurious Recipes:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/hugodarwood/epirecipes

4) Indian Food Recipes Dataset

A dataset focused on Indian cuisine with regional tags and ingredient lists — very useful for authentic Indian coverage.

➡️ Kaggle – Indian Food and Its Recipes Dataset:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/kishanpahadiya/indian-food-and-its-recipes-dataset-with-images

5) Cocktail & Beverage Dataset (CocktailDB or Kaggle variant)

A dataset of drinks, alcoholic and non-alcoholic, with ingredients and instructions — ideal for your beverage generator.

➡️ TheCocktailDB (API/JSON, not Kaggle):
https://www.thecocktaildb.com/
 (free API accessible for download)

Although there isn’t one canonical Kaggle dataset for cocktails with high popularity, you can search for things like:
https://www.kaggle.com/search?q=cocktail+recipes&type=datasets

(Also useful: Food Ingredients & Recipe dataset with images — smaller but handy.)
